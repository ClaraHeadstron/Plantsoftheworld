Zone Africa

African vegetation develops in direct response to the interacting effects of rainfall, temperature, topography, and type of soil; it is further modified by the incidence of fire, human agriculture, and grazing and browsing by livestock. Of the total land area of the continent, forests cover about one-fifth; deserts and their extended margins about two-fifths. woodlands, bushlands, grasslands,and thickets make up the rest. An intimate mosaic of many species has been detailed in many studies of the African tropics. In a few square miles there can be a visible succession from swamp, to grassland, to broad-leaved woodland and grass, to a patch of forest on richer hillside soil, and finally to succulents on a nearly naked rock summit.
