Zone Asia Tropical

A significant feature of South Asian vegetation is the family Dipterocarpaceae (yielding aromatic oils and resins), which is represented there by more than 500 species. Many of the tree species, such as teak, sal, and sandalwood, are highly valuable and are heavily exploited. In areas with a prolonged dry season and less precipitation (e.g., northwestern India, the interior of the Deccan plateau, and the “dry zone” of Myanmar [Burma]), savanna woodland and thorny thickets of acacias and euphorbias are the predominant natural vegetation. In all of the major climate zones of South and Southeast Asia, and particularly on the alluvial plains, much of the natural vegetation has been cleared to make way for agriculture.
